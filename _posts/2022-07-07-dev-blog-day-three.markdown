Unsure how to structure this dev blog... I'm just going to type.

Starting on July 5th, I began with the [Getting Started][intro-getting] and [Intro to React][intro-tut] tutorials on reactjs.org - but first needing to set up my local environment on my new-ish Macbook Air: installing npm and all the various global dependencies, downloading Visual Code, setting up my bash_profile... all the nitty-gritties that I honestly kind of enjoy but recognize can give a false sense of accomplishment. Reminding myself not to get too excited, not to feel so accomplished for taking little steps, but allowing myself to feel some joy for finally rebooting.

I went through the Main Concepts section of [GettingStarted][intro-getting] and looked through many of the Advanced Guides & API Reference pages. I followed along the [Thinking in React section][intro-thinking], developing the FilterableProductTable. I then went through the full [Intro to React tutorial][intro-tut], creating a tic-tac-toe game and honestly feeling a little proud of myself for remembering so much. I then tried my hand at the six improvement ideas in the [Wrapping Up][intro-wrappingup] section, and I did them all! Began to really feel proud of myself because in the past when I did tutorials I usually failed to self-motivate or even think of how to implement improvements on my own. I looked at the [Tutorial Solutions][intro-solutions] provided on the [Small Examples][intro-examples] page and realized my solutions weren't as elegant but I learned from analysis. I then looked through the other examples. Started a [samples github repo][github-samples].

The Builder Book app-tutorial looks very interesting, I may give that a try. It reminded me of my old Udemy, MITx, Pluralsight and other old e-learning website accounts, so I went digging through those but did not find any React tutorials... just a couple dozen others collecting dust.

I logged into my old Heroku account and learned how to deploy the above tic-tac-toe game using a [special buildpack][heroku-buildpack].

I then quickly scanned through Google results of various React & Node tutorials, sifting through many that were either outdated or, in my perspective, poorly constructed or at least not conducive to my learning style. I learned some things scanning through [this video][yt-video1]. Looked on Udemy - some good courses, but let me get through some free content first. Finally stumbled upon this gem of a writer: BezKoder and a recently-published [Node.js Express & PostgreSQL CRUD Rest APIs example with Sequelize][bezkoder-1]. Almost like drinking from a firehose, I determined to re-learn these technologies in a very-well constructed tutorial which still inevitably required me to open a dozen tabs on local environment setup and understanding Sequelize. I omitted a few other articles I had sifted through before this one, noticing other ways to interface with PostgreSQL but desiring to learn this Sequelize ORM to be better equipped for larger projects in the future. I set up Postman and went through the tutorial honestly kind of proud of myself at the speed of understanding and manipulation. Then, I went through other articles of his on pagination, establishing one-to-many and many-to-many relationships with Sequelize, React with Material-UI, authentication, and more. It wasn't all sunshine & rainbows - I had to deal with some versioning issues, database things, deploying to Heroku (my own endeavor separate from his tutorials), git things, and other weird things that weren't worth remembering.

You can view my Bezkoder tutorial implementations on my [github][github-repos]: react-node-app, react-table-pagination-server-side, and react-pagination-material-ui.

None of the above was done in one session; I made sure to take walks, eat food, read and take care of all my other responsibilities and routines I've been establishing over the last few months. But all of the above felt very significant - I remember so much, and just learned so much! I have to almost-constantly stave off stupid intrusive thoughts of "I should be better than this having coded for over six years". While there might be some truth to that, I have to be realistic and self-compassionate. I'm not going to make progress if I keep beating myself up. The best way forward is to treat each day as its own new, unique opportunity. I sometimes like to think that each time I wake up, my soul loaded up a random save file from a random used memory card purchased at EB Games. I have what I have and don't have what I don't have. The only way forward is step-by-step.

After returning from a break, I sifted through several javascript game engines before deciding to keep building small wins: set up my github profile and this github pages website with jekyll. The latter was a pain, dealing with mismatched Ruby versions and figuring out Jekyll which made me feel stupid. These three pages helped a bunch: [one][jekyll-1], [two][jekyll-2], [three][jekyll-3].

And then I wrote these first two blog posts after a nice phone call with a potential business partner. Things are looking up! Blessed to be alive, blessed to remember so much, blessed to be able to learn so much, blessed to even have a computer and electricity and internet and abilities to do any of this! Blessed.

Blessings upon you, reader & fellow traveler. <3
