Hello, world - again. I am just some random developer who, after six years of various employments and a decade of tinkering with dozens of technologies, stepped away from software development due to a complex combination of imposter syndrome, burnout, employment mismanagement, depression, addiction and a pandemic-inspired soul-searching quest.

After a great year of ego & spirit development, losing weight, picking up new hobbies & skills, reading books, traveling and various other adventures I have now resummoned my passion for coding and am restarting my career. As part of this re-journey I decided to start up this simple jekyll-githubpages blog to simply journal some of my progress.

My experience is extremely broad across many languages and frameworks due to many things including a combination of aimlessness and desire to learn a bit of everything, my previous jobs as a consultant where I would be thrown onto a different project every few months, and honestly an underdeveloped or drowned-out willpower to push through challenges or commit to any one thing. I could also scapegoat having mental disorders - which are real, sort of - but I instead determined to understand and overcome the symptoms with a mindful, holistic-based approach. I've made some decent progress there.

My strongest desire has always been game design & development - my stack of notebooks filled with game ideas since middle school can testify - yet for a variety of no-good reasons I never truly pursued it. So, here I am: 27, having failed to launch from an extremely privileged & fortunate launchpad, with various rusty coding skills and still a bit of aimlessness, returning to writing code after a year-long hiatus, blogging with nothing to hide. I could write a very lengthy and somewhat interesting autobiography; perhaps another time.

I have begun with re-learning some things I'm familiar with: Javascript, Node, React, PostgreSQL and deploying to Heroku. I want to develop many of my own ideas of websites, apps, games and web3 projects, as well as contribute to open-source projects and find an organization to co-develop with (I do need money to live, unfortunately). I have to remind myself to be patient while I learn and re-learn much, and to also narrow my scope because I have a tendency to spread myself too thin (and we just don't have the time & energy to do everything).

If anyone is reading: thank you, I guess. I am doing this for my own edification, but perhaps my story will relate to and inspire someone. I say this only because I have found power in relating to other people who are honest & real about their personal and career development. I wish to shine light in the darkness to help others find their way.

I will now write a follow-up post on what I've been coding in these first three days.
