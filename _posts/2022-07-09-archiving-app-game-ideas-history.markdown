Another thing I've been wanting to do forever but never started: archiving and sharing various game & app ideas I've generated over the years, as well as listing out every game I've ever played however futile that may be. Ever since I was bored during class in middle school I've been dreaming and ideating game worlds and apps, scribbling them into notebooks or loose-leaf paper and letting them collect dust. I've managed to make a few, but nothing I'm truly proud of.

For now the ideas are just hastily-typed descriptions. Will add images & screenshots over time.

I'll also randomly upload old notes & list of resources [in the root folder here][folder].

I suppressed my desire to make games for a variety of no-good reasons, listening to other people telling me that it wouldn't be a profitable career, that I'd be overworked, and other very stupid opinions. I attended a two-year long academy in 2011-2013 for software & game development and made a handful of games and prototypes but for a variety of reasons decided to attend a general university for Computer Science and eventually pursue a career in web & app development - which I was skilled at and did enjoy somewhat, but in hindsight maybe I could've made "better" decisions. I still enjoyed my life overall, I'm just dissastisfied with my career progression.

I hid my ideas in notebooks afraid other people would take them but I eventually realized (1) it is very unlikely anyone would take my ideas or create them effectively, (2) if they did then I get to enjoy my game ideas as a player, (3) teams could've recognized my ideas and reached out to me for opportunities, and (4) it's hard to tell people my suppressed passion has been game design when I have nothing online to show for it. Who knows where I'd be if I did - and there's always a possibility my ideas aren't even that great and they wouldn't have taken me anywhere! I've just spent so much of my creative inspiration on imagining huge & complex worlds, imaginary civilizations with cultures and languages, interesting & unique gameplay mechanics (that were later coincidentally implemented in games years later), etc.

I managed to make a few prototypes & little games but spent my life thus-far either playing games, pursuing a somewhat-soulless career consulting in enterprise website development, or experiencing much of what else life has had to offer (which I don't really regret). Part of my dream is to make my ideas real to benefit humanity and just to feel fulfilled doing something with the creative inspiration I've been gifted. Perhaps I pursue Ycombinator or other idea incubators (in hindsight I should've been doing this over the last decade), perhaps I can get hired by a company to provide me with necessary resources, or perhaps I win the lottery and can form my own teams. Is anyone out there? Help wanted.
